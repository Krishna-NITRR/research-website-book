About the Book

Everything a Beginner Needs
to Start Researching

Most students enter research with no roadmap. They copy formats, misread papers, and struggle to find their own voice. This book changes that.

Written for undergraduates, interns, and early-career researchers, it breaks down the entire research process into clear, actionable steps - without the unnecessary jargon.

  • 01
    Finding Your Research Problem
    How to identify meaningful questions worth investigating
  • 02
    Reading Papers Effectively
    Techniques to extract insight without reading everything
  • 03
    Writing Literature Reviews
    How to synthesize existing work and find the gap
  • 04
    Designing Your Methodology
    Choosing the right approach for your research question
  • 05
    Structuring and Writing Your Paper
    From first draft to submission-ready manuscript

About the Author
KM
Krishna Mahawar
B.Tech Mining Engineering · NIT Raipur

Krishna is a mining engineering student at NIT Raipur with a deep interest in research methodology, deep-sea mining, and space resource utilization. He noticed early on that most students struggle not because research is hard - but because no one ever teaches them how research actually works.

This book is his attempt to fix that. Written in the same way he wished someone had explained research to him - clearly, practically, and without unnecessary complexity.
